# CyberAdSpace Films — Anthology Treatment (v1 Draft)

## The Universe

**Setting:** Present-day suburban Florida — Tampa / Cape Coral / Fort Myers / Zephyrhills. Cul-de-sacs, strip malls, palm trees on the median, warehouses with parking-lot gossip, sunset-hour Publix runs. The world is real, not stylized. Every product is a real product in a real cabinet on a real counter.

**Tone:** *Portlandia* meets *Barry* meets an Apple Christmas commercial. Small, funny, warm, occasionally weird. Grounded. Domestic. The comedy comes from characters not the camera. No car chases. No sci-fi. The Cybertruck exists but is treated as a normal (if odd) neighborhood delivery vehicle — never the hero.

**The Connective Tissue — one apartment, one couple, one week.**
Five shorts. Each is 2–3 minutes. Each stands alone. Watched back-to-back in the order below, they form a ~13-minute feature about **Mel and Ray**, a mid-30s Florida couple, and the week they stop sleepwalking through their life. Every brand in the CyberAdSpace family appears as an organic product, service, or beat in that week. Not one billboard. Not one voice-over. Nobody says the word "sponsor."

**The rules of the world (must-follow to keep tone right):**
- No character ever explains a brand. Products just appear where they'd really appear.
- No slow-motion beauty shots of packaging. Camera treats the THC lemonade the same as the milk.
- The Cybertruck delivers stuff to the house across the shorts. It's the closest we get to a "narrator."
- Every short ends on a small emotional beat, not a punchline and not a product.

## The Characters

**Mel (34, she/her)** — Nurse at a Fort Myers clinic. Practical, tired, funny in a dry way. Wants Ray to do something with himself but loves him. Drives an old Corolla. Weekend-project person: cleans out the fridge, replants the succulents, actually reads the mail.

**Ray (36, he/him)** — Between things. Used to install pools; now "consulting." Spends a lot of time on the couch scrolling TikTok. Not a bad guy — a stalled guy. Warm, curious, laughs at his own jokes. Owns four hats.

**Deej (28, they/them)** — Mel's coworker at the clinic. Runs a small side-hustle music page. Shows up in Short 2 and 4.

**Marcus (50s)** — The neighbor across the street. Retired, always mid-project in the driveway. Knows everyone's business without gossiping. Appears in every short.

**Antria (7)** — Marcus's granddaughter, over for the summer. Loud, curious, mishears everything hilariously.

**The Driver** — Wears a CyberAdSpace polo. Never named. Speaks in Short 3. Rings the doorbell in every short.

## Short 01 — "The Fridge" (2:30) — Palm Polish · The Green Oven · Canamo Cafe

*Opening scene. The one you described.*

**Cold open:** Mel comes in from a nurse shift, kicks off her Danskos. Ray is on the couch, TikTok light flickering on his face, a Liquid Death seltzer sweating on the arm of the sofa. He does not look up.

Mel opens the fridge. Beat. She pulls out a **Green Oven THC lemonade**, cracks it, drinks half standing at the counter. On the counter: a foil-wrapped **Canamo Cafe banana bread**, corner torn off, Ray's tooth marks in it. Mel considers the bread. Considers Ray. Cuts a slice. Eats it standing up.

**Doorbell.** Ray doesn't move.

Mel opens the door. It's **The Driver**. Cybertruck at the curb behind him. He hands her a small package. "Palm Polish. Ceramic coat for the Corolla." Mel didn't order it. She turns back to the couch. "Ray." Ray, without looking up: "Belated anniversary. Told 'em not to knock." Mel stares at the back of his head for a long time. Then, quietly, smiles.

She sits down next to him. Steals his seltzer. He wordlessly hands her the remote. **Cut to black.**

**Brands, real and small:** Green Oven lemonade in the fridge. Canamo Cafe bread on the counter. Palm Polish package at the door. Cybertruck at the curb. That's it.

## Short 02 — "Deej's Song" (2:15) — Elevated Remedies · Religion Relief · Antria's Academy

**Next day. Fort Myers clinic breakroom.** Mel and **Deej** on lunch. Deej is on their laptop, headphones half on. "I made a lullaby for my niece. Free tool. Watch this." They flip the laptop around — **Religion Relief** open on screen (the AI music generator, treated as a normal thing you'd have open like Spotify). Deej types "gentle, sleepy, for a kid named Antria." A song plays. It's actually kind of beautiful.

Mel: "That's your side hustle?" Deej: "Nah, side-*side*. The label's called **Elevated Remedies**. Working on a hip-hop EP for it. Faith and hemp." Mel raises an eyebrow. Deej: "Trust." Mel: "I do."

**Cut to:** Marcus's driveway that evening. Antria (7) sitting on the tailgate, listening to the Deej lullaby on Marcus's phone, singing along wrong: *"…and the shine, and the shine, and the SHINE…"* Marcus, sanding something, doesn't correct her. **Cybertruck rolls by** in the background, doesn't stop. Marcus watches it go. Small smile. **Cut to black.**

## Short 03 — "The Driver" (2:45) — CyberAdSpace (the delivery-ad concept itself)

**Mid-day. The Driver's route.** For the first time we see the day from his side. Coffee from a gas station. Loading the truck at a warehouse. He plays a voice memo on the dash: it's his own voice, working out lyrics — *"delivering the future, one QR at a time…"* He winces, deletes it.

He makes three stops we recognize from other shorts (Mel's door, Marcus's driveway, a place we haven't seen yet with a sun-bleached wooden sign: **Williams Produce & More**). At Williams Produce he chats with the old woman running the stand — hemp cookies, coffee, tomatoes. She gives him a tomato. He puts it in the cupholder next to the seltzer.

Last stop of the day: he pulls into the empty lot of an old warehouse, cuts the engine. Sits. Looks at the QR code decal on his own door in the side mirror. Pulls out his phone, opens Notes, types: **"idea: give the shorts a name."** Saves. Drives home. **Cut to black.**

*This is the meta short — the Driver is you, the founder, without ever saying it.*

## Short 04 — "Sunday" (2:30) — The Faith Vault · The Hemp Dispensary

**Sunday morning. Mel's kitchen.** Mel is scrolling on her phone at the counter. Ray shuffles in in pajama pants. On Mel's phone: **The Faith Vault app**, "Scripture Guide" open, a short reading loaded. She reads it silently, no voiceover — we see her lips move slightly. Ray watches her from the doorway without her noticing.

Ray: "You praying?" Mel: "Reading." Ray: "'Bout what?" Mel: "Being patient." Ray, deadpan: "With me?" Mel: "Mm." Long beat. Ray, quietly: "Fair."

**Cut to:** later, Ray in the driveway with **Marcus**, both cracking a **Hemp Dispensary** pre-roll (nothing said, just shown). Marcus: "You gonna get off the couch?" Ray: "Working on it." Marcus: "Working on it is the couch." Ray laughs for the first time in the film. **Cut to black.**

## Short 05 — "Anniversary" (3:00) — Palm Polish (callback) · LustLine · full cast

**Friday night, the week is up.** Mel comes home. The Corolla is in the driveway — freshly detailed, **shining**. She stops in the yard, actually stops. Ray steps out of the house. He's in a real shirt. He hasn't shaved but he's tried. He hands her the keys.

Ray: "Palm Polish. Guy came this afternoon. I stayed off the couch the whole time." Mel: "…all of it?" Ray: "Ninety percent." Mel laughs — a real one.

Inside: Ray has cooked. Not well. It's fine. There's a bottle of something on the table. On Mel's phone, buzzing on the counter, a message notification from an app called **LustLine** — the preview reads *"date-night prompt cards, delivered."* Mel sees it, glances at Ray, doesn't say anything, puts the phone face-down. She's smiling.

**Doorbell.** They freeze. Mel: "Did you order more?" Ray: "…maybe." They both go to the door together. It's **The Driver**. He's holding a small envelope. "Last one for the week." Ray, taking it: "You ever get a night off?" The Driver looks at his truck, at them, at the sunset behind the house. Beat. "Tonight, actually." He walks back to the Cybertruck. Doesn't get in. Just leans on it and watches the sunset with them for a moment.

**Wide shot** of the cul-de-sac. Marcus's porch light comes on. Antria runs across the lawn chasing a lizard. Deej's car pulls into Marcus's driveway with takeout. The Cybertruck sits at the curb, QR code catching the last of the light.

Mel, quietly, to Ray: "This was a weird week." Ray: "Yeah." Beat. "You want to open this?" **Cut to black.**

## The Feature Cut

Watched in order 01 → 02 → 03 → 04 → 05, the shorts form a ~13-minute feature titled **"The Week"** — a small-scale, brand-populated slice of Florida life where a stalled couple, a delivery driver, and a neighborhood full of quiet observers each move one inch forward. Each brand shows up exactly once as itself, in the place it would actually live.
